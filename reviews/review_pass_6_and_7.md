## New chapters added

### Ch08½ "The Portland Office" (1,791 words)

Dr. Sarah Chen's first full on-page therapy session, slotted between Ch08 (Vermont visit) and Ch09 (Morrison archive). Key beats:
- Dr. Chen's office in Portland established (old bottling plant on Commercial Street, small dog bed beside her chair, medium shepherd mix with one blue eye).
- Bilateral stimulation / EMDR protocol introduced with the *pause* command (explicit protocol word chosen to avoid *stop* or *enough* confusion).
- Maya's apartment established as her "safe anchor" for the protocol.
- **New load-bearing memory fragment:** Maya recovers a children's song about a crab coming home with the tide. Not the "hollow shore" ritual song — this is a different song the children sang before Dr. Richard came into the common room. Dr. Chen records Maya humming it for 47 seconds. Kim later plays the recording for the Maine Medical Center survivors; four recognize it. Emma Washington knows the second verse. Michael Hendricks knows the tune ended on a third verse he cannot remember the words to.
- **Dr. Chen's question for Maya's mother:** *"Mom, did Dr. Richard ever contact you about me after I turned eighteen."* Pays off in Ch28 — Frances Chen admits Richard contacted her three times when Maya was 19, 24, and 31. She never told Maya.

### Ch13½ "James" (1,616 words)

Maya's last conversation with James Blackwood before WitSec. A U.S. Marshals safe house outside Augusta. Ninety-minute cleared visit.
- Updates on Danny Morrison, Emma Washington, adult Michael Hendricks.
- James tells Maya about Sarah's last three days (via the knowledge that she was "trying," per Sarah's notebook).
- **New load-bearing artifact:** James gives Maya Sarah's second journal, which he found in her Portland studio three days after her death and hid for ten years. First entry dated April 4, 2011: *"I remember a girl named Maya. I do not remember her last name. I remember that her hand was very small, and warm, and that she had a birthmark shaped like a comma on the inside of her left wrist."*
- Maya looks at her own wrist. The birthmark is there. Small. Shaped like a comma.
- Maya tells James to send her a postcard with no words when his new name is finalized. She will know it is from him. She will not try to find him.

### Ch27½ "The Long Winter" (2,936 words)

Post-Geneva sequence spanning February to May. Five scenes:
1. **February homecoming** — Maya returns to her Boston apartment for the first time in 53 days. The cleaning service's chemical smell. Fourteen hours of sleep. 241 unread messages not yet looked at.
2. **March Vermont visit** — Maya drives to see Michael Hendricks in Carol and Robert's new rental. Michael has own door, own fish tank, is on Hardy Boys #14. Michael and Maya hum the crab song. Michael remembers a third verse the tune ended on. Michael lets Carol put her hand on his for the first time.
3. **March Danny Morrison birthday** — 11th birthday. Hammerhead-shark cake. Danny invited Maya. Anna the ten-year-old disappointed Maya does not have a gun. Mark Morrison's first real laugh.
4. **April Emma Washington painting class** — Emma at 15 teaching five younger survivors. Six-year-old Mia can't get the water on her brush to behave; Emma shows her. Emma afterward: *"The words are *the crab came back with the tide, the tide, the crab came back with the tide.*"* She wrote the third-verse words down in the middle of the night two weeks ago. Maya drove back to Boston humming the full song. Maya thinks: *I am going to be all right.*
5. **May return to Blackwood Island** — Mansion under construction, gray-blue paint replacing Gothic black. Tommy Morrison bench on the path. Sarah's chalk mermaid preserved in the Cathedral cave under glass. **Captain Murphy coda:** Murphy tells Maya he said "some places hold on to things" to Sarah on her last crossing, too. Sarah wrote it down. Sarah acted on it. Murphy: "I have my words now." Dinner with Margaret afterward.

## Note on chapter numbering

New chapters were filed as `ch08a.md`, `ch13a.md`, `ch27a.md` to avoid cascade renaming across three passes. At production time (Pass 8 or post-review), these should be renumbered to integer chapters (Ch08a → Ch09, shifting Ch09-Ch13 to Ch10-Ch14; Ch13a → Ch15; Ch27a → Ch28; Ch28 → Ch32). Renumbering at the very end, once no more chapters will be added, avoids repeated cascading updates to story.md, tracking, and review docs.
