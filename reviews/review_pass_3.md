# Review Pass 3 — The Hollow Shore

**Pass:** 3
**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Reviewer:** LoopReviewerAgent (expansion phase)
**Scope:** Scene-level expansion at five high-value nodes + trait-word thinning.

## Context

Pass 1 and Pass 2 closed all Critical issues and resolved the fatal-flaw genre fracture. The manuscript held a grounded-PI register end to end. But word count (44,436) remained 35-45K short of commercial thriller floor. Pass 3 is the first expansion pass — scene-level texture added at the five nodes where the story needed more oxygen.

## Scene-level expansions landed

### 1. Sarah Blackwood voice (Ch02 + Ch03)

Sarah was previously an absence — a body, a few quoted journal fragments, and a posthumous presence. Pass 3 gives her on-page voice via four Ch02 journal entries + a leather-bound notebook found in Ch03's hidden compartment.

- **Ch02 journal arc:** Maya reads four dated pages on Sarah's desk showing Sarah's memory-recovery arc in parallel with what Maya is about to experience. Sarah found photographs in the attic. Sarah saw adult-Maya in the pictures. Sarah brought the photos to Dr. Torres, who led her through them one at a time. Sarah could not yet say the other children's names. Culminates in the existing "Maya knows" frantic note.
- **Ch03 notebook reveal:** Under the baseboard photographs, Maya finds a leather-bound private notebook (not for Dr. Torres — just for Sarah). Five entries in the final two weeks of Sarah's life. Key new beat: the last entry, dated three days before Sarah's drowning, reads *"I am going to call her. I have her number through a private investigator in Boston."* Sarah was coming for Maya. Sarah was murdered three days before the call.
- **Effect:** Sarah is now an active almost-survivor, not a passive victim. The Ch25 "harvest" reveal lands harder because Sarah was close to saving herself. The Morrison forgery becomes retroactively clearer as a second path Sarah could not complete.
- **Words added:** ~550 across both chapters.

### 2. Emma Washington hospital scene (Ch14)

Emma previously had one line ("The lady from the dreams. You came back for us") and a silent drawing. Pass 3 gives her a quiet, devastating conversation.

- Emma draws a second picture — an adult-sized Emma walking a smaller child out of a cave toward the sun. "When I'm big." First time Maya hears her voice.
- Emma names two additional Fairchild victims: **Ana in Colombia** (burn on her hand), **Jonas in Germany** (Fairchild said Jonas was "not clean enough"). These are new intelligence leads Maya texts to Kim immediately.
- Emma agrees to meet the FBI tomorrow, not today. Maya accepts. Emma is treated as a person making a decision, not a source being harvested.
- **Effect:** Grounds the Ch16 "trauma as investigative superpower" thesis with specific plot-work. Retroactively explains why Trust board membership matters to Maya. Emma's second drawing thematically pays off the Ch28 epilogue ("Emma teaches other, younger children how to paint").
- **Words added:** ~580.

### 3. Kim mid-struggle breakthrough (Ch17)

M22 carry-forward from Pass 1. Kim's identification of Fairchild was previously a past-tense "we got it" announcement. Pass 3 dramatizes the breakthrough.

- Kim has been awake for two and a half days. Maya brings him a sandwich he doesn't look at. At four in the morning Maya finds him muttering at his monitor: *"It's not the volume. It's the grammar."*
- Kim's insight: the Nightingale Fund cipher isn't an anti-forensics checksum, it's a signature handshake. Fairchild's old personal interbank-key signs every transfer.
- Kim needs the key. It's not in modern banking registries because the system was decommissioned in 1998. He hunts through the Morrison 20-year archive for the matching key-block.
- At seven in the morning he finds it: a handwritten bookkeeping sheet the Morrisons' PI had photographed from Fairchild's accountant's trash in 2003. The routing block matches. The identification becomes an earned on-page investigative act.
- **Effect:** The Fairchild reveal stops being a mic-drop and becomes a victory built from two decades of Morrison family work. Fits the grounded-PI register.
- **Words added:** ~320.

### 4. Fairchild Wyoming raid (Ch19)

Previously compressed (1,175 words for a major beat). Pass 3 expands the shrine discovery scene-by-scene.

- Elevator descent now specifically textured (twenty seconds slow travel, stale warm air — reinforcing that the tech is not working).
- Equipment power cords "coiled and zip-tied beside each machine" — proof of non-functional state.
- Folded cards in Fairchild's handwriting beneath each candle dish — Maya does not open them, she knows what's on them.
- All 23 anchor plaques read aloud, not just 3. Each victim's name is spoken with the weight of a memorial.
- **New beat:** Specimen 23, "The Heir," is newer than the others. The candle dish beside it holds an unused candle still in its paper wrapper. The folded card is crisp. "Sarah Blackwood," Maya says. "He was ready for her."
- Maya's reframe: Fairchild wasn't giving his son playmates. He was giving him *a choir*. The anchors are arranged in ranks.
- **Effect:** The shrine scene now carries the emotional weight its plot importance demanded. Sarah-was-ready-for-harvest lands before the Ch25 reveal, as foreshadowing rather than new information.
- **Words added:** ~450.

### 5. Trait-word thinning

Pass 2 carry-forward M4. Global count of precise/precisely/precision/deliberate/deliberately/methodical/methodically/calculated/calculating/calculation/composure/composed was 63 at Pass 1 baseline, ~30 after Pass 1. Pass 3 targeted concentration chapters (Ch02 had 6, Ch06/Ch13/Ch19 had 3 each).

- Ch02: "perfect composure couldn't quite hide" → "fury under Eleanor's aristocratic stillness"; "clinical precision" → "clinical care"; "methodically" → cut
- Ch13: "methodical precision" → "one sheet at a time, unhurried, each motion the shape of a woman who had taken her time"; "deliberate and controlled" → "every movement careful"
- Ch19: "surgical precision" → "by hand"; "methodical" → "patient"; "carefully constructed composure" → "carefully maintained stillness"

Final count: **25** instances, down from 63 baseline (60% reduction). Above the 12-15 budget but the remaining instances are mostly Eleanor's voice-card language ("scalpel precision," "aristocratic composure") which is deliberate per story.md.

- **Words net change:** ~-30 (small trims).

## Remaining carry-forward

- **Word count 46,383** still 34-45K short of commercial thriller floor (80-90K). Pass 4 should continue expansion.
- **Pass 4 candidate expansions:**
  - **New interstitial chapter** between Ch13 and Ch14: Maya's first 12 hours after the hunt ended — driving back to Portland alone, trying to sleep, calling Dr. Sarah Chen for an unscheduled session. Grounds the Ch14 emotional low.
  - **Act 2 Sarah-journal continuation:** Maya re-reads the Ch03 notebook entries at different points in Acts 2-3 as her own memory recovers. Especially Ch16 (therapy reframe), Ch25 (Orchid Room — Sarah's name on "The Heir" plaque would pay off her notebook).
  - **Ethan POV scene:** Once, briefly, inside Act 2 or early Act 3 — perhaps the night Sarah died. Would earn the Ch25 twenty-year-long-con without requiring Ethan to narrate it at the Orchid Room.
  - **Dr. Richard interrogation expansion (Ch15):** More interiority on Maya's fear-of-being-wrong (Marcus Williams callback already seeded).
  - **Morrison family on-page more than once:** Currently Mark Morrison only appears in Ch07 and Ch17. Pass 4 could add a Ch08 or Ch22 scene where the Morrison 20-year archive is handed over in person.
- **Trait-word accumulation still above budget** (25 vs. 12-15). Remaining instances are mostly voice-card-correct Eleanor references. Pass 4 should verify any non-Eleanor uses.
- **Consecutive-High-temperature streaks** not yet addressed. Act 1 (Ch02-06) still runs High-High-High-High-High. Pass 4 could insert a low-temp relief beat in the Ch03 dinner aftermath or Ch05 morning.

## Pass 3 Convergence Verdict

**Converged for Pass 3 scope** — the five targeted expansions all landed. Mechanical baselines (em-dashes, tags, endings, "suddenly") held steady under the additions.

**Not converged for publisher-readiness** — word count still well short. Pass 4+ should be a dedicated expansion campaign (Ethan POV scene, Act 2 interstitial chapters, expanded Morrison family on-page, Sarah-journal continuation through Acts 2-3).

The book is now in substantially better shape than the original 41K draft: every Critical issue resolved, grounded-PI register holding, Sarah as active almost-survivor, Emma as voice-bearing character, Fairchild's raid emotionally earned, Kim's breakthrough dramatized. Pass 4 can prioritize expansion with confidence in the existing structure.
