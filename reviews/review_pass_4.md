## Rationale

Pass 3 added 1,947 words across five scene nodes (Sarah voice, Emma scene, Kim breakthrough, Fairchild raid, trait-word thinning). ## Expansions landed

### 1. Ch14 opening: drive back + 2 AM therapy + failure to sleep (+1,007 words)

Ch14 previously opened cold on the FBI Portland field office. Pass 4 adds the four hours after Ch13's tarmac scene: Maya's drive south through Maine, dialing her mother's number twice without letting it ring, arriving at her hotel at 00:11, calling Dr. Sarah Chen's after-hours line.

Key beats:
- Dr. Chen opens with *"Stop apologizing and tell me where you are"* — voice-card on-target, warm-specific, not sitcom-therapist.
- Maya articulates that she has felt nothing on the tarmac. Dr. Chen reframes: *"You are not supposed to feel anything yet. The body closes some doors. The body keeps you alive. In the morning things will open, and they will hurt, and we will work on them together."*
- Maya names the Marcus Williams fear directly: *"Being wrong. About them. The way I was wrong once before."* Dr. Chen grounds this: Maya has the files. She is not projecting.
- Maya does not sleep. Arrives at the FBI field office at 7:15, second person through the doors.

**Effect:** The Ch14 interrogation victory + parents phone call both land with emotional floor underneath them. Previously the Ch14 opening jumped from external win (Eleanor cracks) to private devastation (parents confess) without the private interior that earns the pivot.

### 2. Ch15 Marcus Williams deepening (+224 words)

The Marcus Williams thread (Pass 1 M9) was reinstated as one-sentence references in Ch14 and Ch15. Pass 4 gives it specific content during Dr. Richard's interrogation.

Key beats:
- Marcus Williams's physical description (height, scar, cleft chin). The hundred-yard Columbia alley glance. A sister of the victim pointing: *"that one."* Eleven-month sentence. Real killer's deathbed confession (pancreatic cancer, hospice).
- Maya's post-release pro bono work that her agency could not afford. Marcus Williams's heart attack at 47. Fourteen months out of prison. His mother not speaking to Maya at the funeral.
- Active test during the Ch15 interrogation: Maya measures her certainty about Richard against her certainty about Marcus Williams. Finds the difference. Marcus Williams's look across the alley had been confusion. Richard Blackwood is performing.

**Effect:** The interrogation scene now has an active internal check running. Fear-of-being-wrong is no longer a flag; it is a working muscle Maya uses to verify her perception of Dr. Richard.

### 3. Sarah journal callbacks (Ch16 +283w, Ch25 +108w)

Pass 3 gave Sarah on-page voice in Acts 1. Pass 4 pays that voice off at two pivotal moments in Acts 2-3.

**Ch16 (therapy reframe):** After Dr. Chen names Maya's past as her "superpower," Maya opens Sarah's notebook for the first time since she took it from the hidden compartment. Reads an entry dated twelve days before Sarah's drowning:

> *Dr. Torres asked me today what I would say to Maya if I could. I said I would say I was sorry I didn't remember sooner... Maya will remember better than me. She always did. She was the one who wrote down where we had been in her notebook. I was the one who drew the mermaid on the wall.*

Maya realizes Sarah's promise was not about the cave. It was about this — the case file with twenty-three names. Maya sleeps six hours, the longest stretch in nine days.

**Ch25 (Orchid Room):** When Ethan confesses that Sarah was the "vessel" Fairchild had been preparing, Maya touches Sarah's notebook through her jacket. She does not take it out. Recalls the final entry — *I am going to call her. I have her number through a private investigator in Boston.* The ground retroactively shifts: Sarah had been three days from her own rescue. Sarah had almost saved herself. Sarah had started the phone call Maya had ended up answering.

**Effect:** Sarah is now an arc that tracks from attic discovery to final-entry phone-call attempt, and the reader carries that arc into the Orchid Room. The Ch25 Sarah-as-vessel reveal lands harder because Sarah almost escaped.

### 4. Ethan letter inside Ch27 briefcase (+1,144 words)

Ethan's twenty-year long-con was the biggest "told not shown" residue after Pass 1's grounded-PI reframe. Pass 4 gives Ethan voice through a twelve-page handwritten letter he clipped inside the briefcase lid, addressed *Ms. Maya Chen, personal*.

Key content of the letter:
- Ethan was 12 the night Tommy died. 13 when Fairchild's scouts found him at a Halifax children's home. Fairchild told him his family was dead (a lie Ethan did not contradict because he had no proof).
- Fairchild sat him in a conservatory of orchids, told him he would have a name and a family and an education. Also told him that children who had recently come into his care sometimes had bad dreams about things that had not actually happened, and that if the dreams became unbearable, he had people who could help. Ethan understood the threat. He smiled and said he had no dreams.
- Ethan's twenty-year decision to stay alive inside the house, learn everything, build a record, wait until there was someone in the world who could finish what he started.
- He spent ten years watching Sarah. She started remembering six weeks before Richard killed her. Ethan tried to warn Dr. Torres indirectly through a fabricated client referral. The letter reached her two days late. Sarah drowned on a Tuesday.
- The Morrison forgery was routed through a man who worked for Fairchild's Boston foundation and owed Ethan favors. The fifty thousand was the minimum to get Maya's failing agency to take the case. It was from Ethan's personal account.
- Signed simply *Ethan*.

**Effect:** The twenty-year long-con is now earned. Ethan becomes a specific person who made a decision at thirteen and kept it for twenty-four years. The Ch27 *Thank you. / Ethan* final message has real weight now. Maya reads the letter three times before the Geneva raid kicks off; she folds it and carries it against her sternum inside her jacket, finger tapping once against the paper through fabric. The letter is not a plot device; it is a portrait.
