# Review Pass 5 — The Hollow Shore

**Pass:** 5
**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Reviewer:** LoopReviewerAgent (expansion phase — investigation texture)
**Scope:** Dramatize beats the book previously summarized. Six chapter expansions targeting the Act 1-2 investigation arc.

## Rationale

After Pass 4, manuscript was 49,149 words with every Critical issue resolved and grounded-PI register holding. The gap to publisher-readiness (80-90K) was structural: the book's investigation arc — Ch07 through Ch13 — ran mostly as montage. Phone calls where scenes were needed. Two-sentence transitions where emotional beats belonged. Pass 5 converts six of these summaries into scenes.

Strategic Reframe held through all additions: no SF language introduced, no genre drift, grounded-PI register reinforced via specific physical details (paper archives, ceramic dishes, cave helipads, hotel parking lots).

## Expansions landed

### 1. Ch04 afternoon relief beat + Margaret Swift note (+737 words)

The Ch02-06 five-in-a-row High-temperature streak broken. Maya stews alone in her locked room after the morning confrontation. Quiet interior while the family lets her wait. She makes an inventory list on the back of a page of Sarah's journal: *what I have, what they have, what I need.* Underlines *witness.*

**New plot device:** Margaret Swift slips a note under Maya's door. *The main telephone in the library is a modern cordless. He cut the wire from the kitchen extension. He did not cut the library base. The study has a key to the boathouse in the middle desk drawer. The boat is fueled. The tide is out at four o'clock. I am sorry. I should have said something years ago.* Maya does not get to use the note — Ch05 events overtake the plan — but she keeps it. Pays off in Ch11 when Margaret comes to the cave. Maya shows her the note. Margaret: "Keep it. Put it with the evidence. Or don't." Silence broken three times now: 1998 to Holcomb's PI, Ch04 note, Ch11 cave.

### 2. Ch08 on-page Vermont visit (+957 words)

Video call replaced with in-person six-hour drive to Burlington. Key specific beats:
- Carol Hendricks's statement that she stopped printing missing posters four years ago. "I am telling you that because I want you to know that we stopped for four years."
- Michael Hendricks's preserved bedroom: Hardy Boys paperbacks with bookmark in chapter four of *The Melted Coins*, dried fish tank, Red Sox poster, fifteen years of Sunday cleaning.
- **New physical artifact:** Michael's fifth-grade ceramic dish inscribed *FOR DAD. FROM THE HOLLOW SHORE.* First concrete object linking "hollow shore" to victim children (prior references were verbal testimony). Carol presses the dish into Maya's hands. Maya takes it in a padded evidence envelope.
- Six-hour drive back to Boston without radio.
- **First on-page phone call to Frances Chen:** Maya asks if her mother remembers a boy named Tommy. The silence on the line is "the kind Maya had heard across an interview table in Boston a hundred times." Frances: *"I don't know what you mean, honey."* Plants Ch14's confession.

### 3. Ch09 Morrison family archive delivery (+1,171 words)

Phone call with Patricia Valdez replaced with in-person scene. Mark Morrison drives up in a rented U-Haul with **411 boxes** of twenty-three years of Morrison family private investigation. Labeled *1998: Dock Area Canvass, Pemaquid to Bar Harbor, PI Firm 1 (Holcomb & Associates).* Etc.

**Key new plant:** Box 1, folder 3, holds Holcomb's 1998 interview note with Margaret Swift at the Bar Harbor dock. Margaret (young woman, mid-thirties at the time) told Holcomb she had nothing to say. Holcomb wrote: *Interviewee visibly fearful... Stated, quote, "Some places hold on to things." Unquote.* Coastal-community echo of Captain Murphy's Ch01 line. Margaret's silence-breaking now a twenty-three-year arc.

Mark Morrison on-page for the first time as a full character. His exhausted *"I want to be finished"* rather than *"I want to be a star"* testimony moment. Patricia Valdez projects case: *"United States v. the Collectors' Circle, all fifty-two of them, by the time we are done."* Agent Kim photographs every index page.

### 4. Ch11 ferry opening + Margaret Swift callback (+366 words)

The return-to-the-island ferry journey now has texture. Detective Park meets Maya at the Bar Harbor terminal with a thermos. They stand at the rail through the first half of the crossing without speaking. Maya takes Sarah's notebook out, reads the Dr. Torres entry. Park: "You ready for this?" Maya: "No." Park: "Good answer."

Maya mentions Sarah's "I am going to call her" entry. Park is quiet. *"Then we do right by her."*

Later in the chapter, when Margaret Swift reveals the cave entrance, Maya references the Ch04 note she kept for three weeks. Shows Margaret the note. Margaret: *"Keep it. Put it with the evidence. Or don't."* Maya: *"You broke the silence for me first. Then for Sarah. Now for them."* Threading Margaret's arc cleanly.

### 5. Ch12 four-hour extraction + Maine Medical Center (+1,257 words)

The 18-child rescue was a single sentence. Now four hours of named extraction work:
- Emma Washington needs seven minutes on the stone floor before she'll set down her chalk.
- Michael Hendricks (adult, 27 years old, grown into a thin quiet man) saying "Thank you, ma'am. I know this is your job."
- A girl named Lisa who doesn't know how to walk more than ten feet without stopping to listen for the next instruction.
- Emma's *"I cannot go outside. The man said the sun will hurt us."* Maya's *"He was trying to make you small. I am trying to bring you home."*
- Helipad sequence: Ashley Brennan (10, two weeks under) on the first medevac, Marcus Hale (9, three weeks under) with her, Sophia Martinez (8, five days) holding a paramedic's thumb.
- Tyler Park, four years in the caves, getting his other hand held by a paramedic saying "You are safe. You are safe. You are safe."

**Maine Medical Center** has cleared the fourth-floor pediatric wing. Mrs. Washington flew from Hartford. Ashley Brennan's mother drove eight hours to beat the helicopter. The Martinez family walked. Emma meets her mother: *"You look like my mother, but older."* Mrs. Washington: *"I am your mother, Emma. I got older while you were away."*

Ashley Brennan's mother hugs Maya for five seconds without speaking. Maya sits in a hallway plastic chair for a long time.

### 6. Ch13 post-arrest interiority + Park + drive back (+759 words)

Between Richard's tarmac arrest and Maya calling her mother, Pass 5 adds:
- Maya at the chain-link fence waiting for anger / satisfaction / relief. Nothing comes. Dr. Chen's *be a body* mantra surfaces as interior voice. *Stand. Breathe. Feel your feet on the ground.*
- Detective Park arrives (she'd been in a car twenty minutes behind Martinez). Non-cop question: *"Are you going to be all right."* Maya: *"I do not know yet."* Park: *"When you know, you call me. Not Martinez. Not Chen. Me."*
- Captain Murphy reported at the ferry dock shaking Mark Morrison's hand and standing at the rail with him for ten minutes. Not overheard.
- Drive back to Bar Harbor with Martinez. Supplemental statement at the Bar Harbor police station. Eleanor visible through a glass panel in a conference room with her attorney.
- Maya alone in the Holiday Inn parking lot at eight in the evening. Dials her mother. Hangs up on the third ring before Frances can answer. Drives south.

**Continuity chain with Ch14's Pass 4 drive scene:** now reads "She had dialed her mother's number twice. Once from the Bar Harbor hotel parking lot... Once somewhere past Ellsworth." The Bar Harbor → Portland drive chains seamlessly.
