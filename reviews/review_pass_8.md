## Deliverable B — Publisher & Reviewer Panel

### 1. Acquisitions Editor (Big Five imprint)

At 63,322 words this is still a commercial pass for a Big Five adult psychological-thriller hardcover — the floor is 80K, sweet spot 85-95K. Chapter word-count distribution is visibly uneven: Ch02=3,217, Ch08=3,107, Ch14=3,186, Ch27=3,013, Ch27a=3,446, Ch28=2,825 — six chapters over 2,800 — alongside a valley of sub-1,500s in the late middle (Ch16=1,189, Ch18=1,055, Ch21=1,052 [+6 from this pass's fix], Ch22=1,087, Ch24=1,000). The expansion passes grew the scenes the author wanted to grow, not the scenes the market needs grown.

Genre fracture is substantially better but not gone. Ch19 reads correctly as a shrine visit rather than a lab breach thanks to the grounding lines; Ch25 Ethan's `"It's a flower, Maya. It's a very expensive flower that will die when nobody waters it. The rest of his beliefs worked the same way"` is the line this book needed to plant. However, Ch19's set-piece is still a cashmere-sweater octogenarian listening to Bach on a Persian-rug pressure plate. Reframed as ritual it works thematically, but the cinematic furniture is still Dan Brown's. A careful Big Five editor will still catch the seam.

Credible comps now: Shari Lapena, Riley Sager, Lisa Jewell. Drop French/Flynn/Atkinson from the query — the prose doesn't sustain French's paragraph-level shimmer and Flynn's acid is absent. **Verdict: pass at this length; request on an 85K manuscript with Ch19 reframed and Ch22-24 expanded.**

### 2. Developmental Editor

The arc now executes. Ch08a's mother-question setup → Ch28 payoff is load-bearing across fifteen chapter-units. Ch20 is properly in present tense. Ch27a's Long Winter is the prosecution/integration phase the old Ch28 could not carry alone. Sarah now has three on-page voice sources (attic pages Ch02, hidden notebook Ch03, second sketchbook Ch13a), and with this pass's Ch27a additions, the sketchbook and comma-birthmark pay off. The book earns its ending.

But expansion introduced a **victory-lap problem**. Ch27a is 3,446 words of the protagonist receiving gratitude (Michael, Danny's family, Emma, Murphy). Ch28 is 2,825 words of ribbon-cutting attendees thanking her. The prosecution phase proper is handled offstage in a single paragraph; the competency hearings, counter-offensives, leaked-file scares, and Fairchild trial are all skipped. The book is now adequately long but emotionally front-weighted toward closure.

Second dev-level concern: **Ethan's long con still does not plausibly earn itself.** Ch27's twelve-page letter is beautifully written but is first-person confession papering over the coincidence staircase. `"I did not forge Eleanor's stationery myself. I routed the forgery through a man who worked for Fairchild's Boston foundation and who owed me favors for reasons I would rather not put on paper"` — that parenthetical is the author acknowledging the machinery is too convenient and waving it past. An Ethan POV chapter (even a short Ch23½ interstitial) would carry more weight than the letter. Third concern: Dr. Richard captured Ch13 and returns only via Ch15 interrogation — the best antagonist is off the board for fifteen chapters; Fairchild (introduced Ch17, raided Ch19) is a mid-boss too late to carry the villain slot.

### 3. Copy Editor

Mechanical baseline is rescued. **Zero em-dashes** (verified). **Zero "suddenly."** Aphoristic-ending budget held (Ch06 "wept without making any sound at all", Ch12 "did not get up for a long time", Ch19 "did not move for a long time", Ch25 "did not speak yet", Ch26 "a problem someone else was going to have to solve", Ch28 stone-skipping). Forbidden-tag grep returns 9 real violations after this pass's fixes — down from 85 at baseline and down from 20 at review-start. Credible remediation.

New habits the expansion introduced that will slow a copyedit:
- **Monosyllabic Yes/Yeah/Okay/Good ladders** — 68 instances across 16 chapters, concentrated in Ch13a (7), Ch14 (9), Ch27 (7), Ch27a (9), Ch28 (7). Denis Johnson / Cormac McCarthy tic. When four consecutive paragraphs are under ten words, the book stops sounding like people and starts sounding like a translation. Signature or tic? Requires style-sheet decision.
- **Missing question marks on rhetorical questions** — `"How is your sleep."` / `"You okay."` / `"You still there."` Inconsistent enough that a house copyeditor will flag every instance and halt production until style-sheet clarifies.

Paragraph-rhythm drift: **Ch28's 35-line attendees cascade**, each party in its own paragraph, flattens into a casting-call list. Scene, not roster. **Ch27a has 8 narrative paragraphs over the 5-sentence cap** — some intentional rhythm, some should split.

### 4. Genre-Savvy Beta Reader

The emotional middle lands now. Ch08a was the scene that surprised me most — Dr. Chen was a cliché last pass, and she's now a person with a dog-bed in her office who says *"Mom, did Dr. Richard ever contact you about me after I turned eighteen. That is the question."* and gives Maya EMDR with a *pause* protocol word. The crab song recovered as a non-traumatic fragment — memory as joy, not horror — is the most humane moment in the book. Ch12 Emma at the cave mouth. Ch14 one-question phone call. Ch20 Cathedral mermaid. Ch27a Michael humming the third verse. I cried at three of those.

Where did I still put the book down? **Ch19.** Fairchild in cashmere listening to Bach in a glass mountain lair with a Persian-rug pressure plate — I did not believe him as a human being, and the new "it's a chapel, not a lab" disclaimer is telling-rather-than-showing. **Ch22-24.** Airport-thriller synopsis strip. Ch24 is the shortest chapter at 1,000 words and carries the Toronto thesis-advisor reveal — it reads like a Wikipedia entry. **Ch27 letter.** Earnest, long, and in the same voice reading itself aloud: *"When I pressed the bird into your hand I was twelve years old and I thought I was going to die within the hour."* I kept feeling the author's hand on the tiller.

The epilogue works. Ch27a's Captain Murphy bench, Michael's third verse, Emma's mermaid under glass, the father throwing the stone in Ch28 — all earn themselves. But the ribbon-cutting victory parade before the stone is excessive. I wanted Ch28 to be Maya and her parents alone on the gravel, not Maya on a porch receiving gratitude from sixteen parties.

### 5. Adversarial Reviewer

The genre fracture is not gone. It has been painted over with one retcon sentence per set-piece and the scaffolding shows. `"It was a chapel, not a lab"` in Ch19 is a disclaimer glued onto a scene originally written as SF. The Wyoming sub-level chamber is still a circular 30-foot room containing "twenty-three smaller units, each the size of a kneeling child" and "a tall glass pod" holding a "stunningly lifelike mannequin of a ten-year-old boy with sandy hair" suspended in "still, pale blue fluid." The book is now narrating that this is a ritual space. It still looks like the lair. The fix is a coat of paint over a structural beam. A hostile reviewer will call this out in one sentence.

The Southern drawl was lip-service in the action climax before this pass's Ch27:23 fix, and even after the fix the book's drawl inventory remains suspicious. Total drawl instances: nine. Six of those are variations of `"Lord have mercy"`. The `"What in the Sam Hill"` / `"Sure as I'm standin'"` / `"fixin' to"` vocabulary appears in specific set-pieces (Ch11, Ch13, Ch21) like a checklist rather than a voice. At 63K words the book does not *sound* Southern under stress; it *flags* Southern at moments where the author remembered to.

The writing still embarrasses itself in specific places. **Ch27's letter** — `"I spent twenty years copying his papers. I spent ten years watching Sarah"` reduces a quarter-century of moral compromise to a Stephen-King epigram. **Ch27a Tommy-Morrison sandwich motif over-architected** — invented in Ch26 as a single grace note, by Ch28 it is the brass-plaque memorial line. Motif visibly architected in real time. **Ch28 Patricia "told not to thank Maya"** — sentence tells us Maya is the kind of hero who refuses to be thanked by itemizing the refusal. Aphoristic thesis-sentence tic in administrative disguise. **Ch19 twenty-three-specimen liturgical call-and-response** — staged, theatrical, paid for with character rather than earned by it.

Is 63K a different class of problem than 41K was? Yes, worse class. 41K was a short manuscript. 63K is a novella pretending to be a novel by stretching the seams the author wanted to stretch. The expansion went disproportionately into Ch08a, Ch13a, Ch14, Ch27, Ch27a, Ch28 — emotional-integration and closure chapters. Ch16 is still 1,189 words. Ch18 is 1,055. Ch21 is 1,058. Ch22 is 1,087. Ch24 is 1,000. The middle-Act-3 investigative throughline was not expanded, because it is the least emotionally rewarding territory to write. A hostile reviewer will say: *"The author wrote the scenes they wanted to write and the rest reads like treatment notes."* The fix from here is not another pass. It is a targeted expansion of Ch16-Ch24 at 1,200 words per day with nobody telling the author to protect the crab song.
