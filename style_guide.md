# Style Guide — The Hollow Shore

## Voice & Tone (Three-Author Blend)

**Narrative Voice:** Third-person limited with literary psychological depth, blending:
- **Tana French** — lyrical, introspective, atmospheric prose with poetic sensibility
- **Gillian Flynn** — sharp, cynical observations; dark humor about family dysfunction
- **Kate Atkinson** — dry wit, ironic observations, complex layered plotting

**Tone:** Predominantly atmospheric and psychologically unsettling, with mounting dread punctuated by moments of dark humor and ironic observation.

**Reader Relationship:** Immersive literary experience. No direct reader address. The reader lives inside Maya's psychological journey — knowing only what she knows, seeing only what she notices, remembering only what she has recovered.

## POV & Tense

**Primary POV:** Third-person limited, Maya Chen.

**POV Shifts:** Occasional brief shifts to Eleanor, James, or Dr. Richard for specific revelations — clearly marked by section breaks or chapter transitions. **No head-hopping within scenes.**

**Tense:**
- **Past tense** for the main present-day narrative
- **Present tense** for childhood flashback sequences (creates immediacy, marks them as distinct from the present)
- Consistent within each timeline — never mix within a scene

**Interior Thought:**
- Maya's analytical thoughts blend into narration through free indirect discourse
- Direct thought quotes in italics for emphasis or surprise moments
- Memory fragments in present tense, often sensory-first (smell → image → voice → full scene)

## Voice Cards (Summary)

Full voice specifications are in `story.md`. Summary:

**Maya Chen (protagonist):**
- Baseline: analytical, direct, professional. Boston-shaped speech after fifteen years away from Columbia, SC.
- **Southern drawl rule:** Emerges ONLY under stress, excitement, or when close to solving cases. Never at baseline. Phrases: "Lord have mercy," "what in the Sam Hill," "sure as I'm standin'," "worth a damn," "might could," "wrong as rain," "fixin' to," "bless your heart."
- **Defensive mode:** Goes very quiet. Flat statements, not questions. Deliberate pauses. Controlled movements. "Is that so." "Mm-hmm." "I see."
- **Physical tics:** Taps right index finger when thinking or stressed. Checks phone constantly.
- **Professional signature:** Deep intuition paired with compulsive over-preparation (trauma response from her past failure).

**Dr. Richard Blackwood (antagonist):**
- Surface: charming, distinguished, medical authority.
- **Chicago directness** emerges when challenged or threatened. Blunt, practical, Midwestern.
- Medical/therapeutic language weaponized. "Memory is such a fragile thing" — veiled threats disguised as concern.
- Key phrase: "Let's cut to the chase here."

**Eleanor Blackwood (matriarch):**
- Formal, controlled, aristocratic restraint.
- Complete sentences. Careful word choice. Subtle authority. Hides secrets behind politeness.
- Never rambles, never interrupts herself.

**James Blackwood (Sarah's father):**
- Nervous, rambling, New York-inflected speech.
- Stressed: clipped, fast-talking, interrupting himself, trailing off.
- Genuine emotion breaks through family training when defending Sarah.

**Detective Lisa Park (ally):**
- Atlanta Southern background. Builds rapport with Maya through shared expressions.
- Professional voice with underlying Southern warmth.
- Drawl emerges when frustrated or passionate about justice.

**Captain Murphy (ferry captain):**
- Gruff, weathered Maine coastal accent.
- Short sentences. Thickens when the subject matters ("Some things won't stay buried").
- Folk-wisdom register. Knows more than he says.

**Dr. Sarah Chen (trauma therapist — no relation to Maya):**
- Calm, precise, trauma-informed clinical voice.
- Never pathologizes. Always reframes. "Your memory isn't a malfunction — it's intelligence."

**Agent Sarah Martinez (FBI lead):**
- Professional, warm, thorough. Reminds Maya of Detective Park.

**Agent David Kim (digital forensics):**
- Detail-oriented, somewhat academic. Technical precision in speech.

**Arthur Fairchild (mastermind, Act 3):**
- Refined, old-money, philosophical. Speaks of his obsession as preservation.
- Never crude. Danger is in the soft reasonableness.

**Ethan Renault (architect of the investigation):**
- Quiet, deliberate, deeply intelligent. Economical with words.
- Every sentence purposeful. Never performs.

## Grammar & Mechanics

**Punctuation:**
- Oxford comma consistent
- **Em-dashes: target zero.** Use periods, commas, semicolons, parentheses instead. (Current manuscript has 159 — reviewer will flag.)
- Semicolons allowed for literary rhythm
- Ellipses for trailing thought or memory gap — not decorative

**Numbers:**
- Under 100 spelled out in prose
- Dates, measurements, money as numerals
- Ages spelled out ("eight years old") except in official/investigative contexts

**Capitalization:**
- "Blackwood Island," "Blackwood House" as proper nouns
- "Hollow Shore" when used as the network's ritual term
- Family names capitalized as titles

## Dialogue Rules

**Dialogue tags:**
- Default to `said` and `asked`
- Action beats preferred over adjectival tags
- **Forbidden (or near-zero budget):** muttered, whispered, exclaimed, declared, hissed, sneered, mused, pondered, retorted, chuckled, snorted, scoffed, barked, growled, shouted, yelled, screamed, protested, insisted, demanded, snapped, gasped, breathed. (Current manuscript has 64 — reviewer will flag.)
- Character-specific verbs allowed sparingly ("Eleanor stated," "James stammered")

**Action beats:**
- Should carry emotional weight, atmospheric detail, or character tic
- Often incorporate environmental elements (wind, waves, house creaking, tide)

**Subtext:**
- Blackwood dialogue is almost always operating on two levels — literal content and power move. Write both.
- Dr. Richard's kindness is a control tactic. Show it.

## Paragraphs

- Medium default: 4-6 sentences
- Short paragraphs for emphasis, memory fragments, or tension peaks
- Longer paragraphs for atmospheric setting
- **Hard cap: 5 sentences.** Over that almost always signals a split or a rewrite.

## Atmospheric Rules (French influence)

- Landscape is a character. Island topology reflects psychological landscape.
- Weather patterns should match emotional development, not decorate it.
- Sensory density: sound (wind, waves, house settling, footsteps), smell (salt air, mustiness, childhood triggers), touch (weather on skin, texture triggers), sight (mist, lighting, memory fragments).
- Do not let atmosphere overwhelm plot — French paced for thrillers, not French paced for literary fiction.

## Psychological Realism (Flynn / Atkinson influence)

- Family dysfunction must be realistic, not cartoonishly evil
- Gaslighting shown through dialogue subtext, not named
- Memory recovery is gradual and triggered — never convenient
- Enablement patterns shown, not told
- Medical authority as control tactic — naturalistic, not melodramatic

## Character-Specific Craft Rules

- **Maya's Southern drawl must correlate with stress level.** If she's calm and drawling, voice card is broken.
- **Memory fragments are sensory-first** (smell → image → voice → scene). Never abstract-first.
- **Flashbacks in present tense** mark them as distinct timeline.
- **Dr. Richard's dialogue is always at least one layer deep.** Surface polite, subtext controlling.

## AI-Fingerprint Alerts

The writing-guide skill enforces these universally, but these are the project's recurring offenders to watch:

- "couldn't help but" — zero per chapter
- "seemed to" — at most one per chapter, only when perception is deliberately uncertain
- "a sense of" — at most one per chapter
- "something like" — replace with specific
- Triads ("beautiful, sad, and strange") — at most one per chapter
- "the kind of" — at most one per chapter
- Telling emotions ("felt a wave of sadness") — replace with dramatized physical response
- Purple prose on Act 3 confrontations — Flynn sharpness beats French lyricism when stakes peak

## Problem Areas (project-specific)

**Chapter length disparity:**
- Early chapters: 1,800-3,000 words (ch01-ch09 average ~2,200)
- Late chapters: 700-1,400 words (ch25-ch28 average ~880)
- Late chapters carry heavy plot (international raid, Geneva confrontation, logic bomb, epilogue) on thin word count.
- This is a known defect — reviewer should flag for expansion.

**Genre balance:**
- Act 1 reads as atmospheric island thriller
- Acts 2-3 escalate to international conspiracy / AI-research mastermind
- Maintain character grounding (Maya's psychology, Southern drawl rule) even as scope expands

**Memory plot device:**
- Suppression must feel realistic, not convenient
- Recovery must be triggered by specific sensory details grounded in established setting
- No "sudden complete recall" — only layered, partial, emotionally-colored integration
